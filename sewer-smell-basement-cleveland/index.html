Professional help for basement sewer odor

A sewer smell in the basement can be frustrating and may point to a drain problem, standing water issue, trap concern, or another plumbing-related condition. Common causes of sewer smell in a basement

Common causes may include drain trap problems, infrequently used drains, drain or vent issues, standing water conditions, and deeper sewer or line concerns. Because basement odor can have more than one source, diagnosis starts with what is visible, accessible, and most likely based on the symptoms described.

When to schedule a sewer smell evaluation

  • Odor coming from a floor drain or nearby plumbing fixture
  • Basement smell after storms or heavy drain use
  • Recurring odor that comes and goes
  • Basement drain issues combined with backups or slow flow
  • A smell strong enough to affect finished basement areas
